2014

Single jersey

The 2014 Team Canada jersey was one of the first jerseys I ever designed, creating it prior to receiving any formal design education. My goal with the jersey was to create something abstract that would feel unique and have a strong Canadian identity.

2016

Single jersey

In 2016, the Qudditch World Cup expanded to 21 nations, with jersey design trends leaning to a lot of solid colours and curved shapes. I aimed to break the trends and give Team Canada their own style that would contrast well with their opponents, both making it easier to distinguish themselves on the pitch and to create a design statement.

2018

Full home & away kits

The goal of the 2018 kit was to incorporate a wider range of Canadian symbols and to create a way to represent the individual Canadian Quidditch teams. The main leaf crest features a number of iconic Canadian symbols including a canoe, fleur-de-lis, and the Royal Crown. It also features a symbol that represents each of the players' regular season teams.
